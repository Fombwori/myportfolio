Cybersecurity Courses

A showcase of advanced cybersecurity modules, projects, and research undertaken during MSc studies.

Launching into Cyber Security

Introduces students to the fundamentals of cybersecurity, including its history, core concepts, threat landscape, and current trends. It provides a foundation for understanding how digital systems are targeted and defended, preparing learners for more specialized topics.

Network Security

Focuses on safeguarding data as it moves across networks by studying protocols, firewalls, intrusion detection systems, VPNs, and encryption. It equips students with the knowledge to design and implement secure communication systems that resist attacks and unauthorized access.

Principles of Digital Forensics and Cyber Law

Explores methods of investigating cyber incidents by collecting and preserving digital evidence while adhering to legal and ethical frameworks. It emphasizes the role of law in governing cybercrime, data protection, and digital rights to ensure that investigations hold up in court.

Security and Risk Management

Examines how organizations identify, assess, and mitigate cybersecurity risks. Students learn risk management frameworks, compliance requirements, and governance practices that align security with business objectives to maintain resilience against evolving threats.

Secure Systems & Development

Secure Systems Architecture: Covers the principles of designing robust, secure computing infrastructures, from hardware to cloud environments. It emphasizes layered defense strategies, system hardening, and architectural patterns that minimize vulnerabilities and enable trusted operations.

Secure Software Development: Teaches best practices for building applications with security in mind throughout the software development lifecycle (SDLC). It includes topics such as threat modeling, secure coding, vulnerability testing, and integrating security controls into agile and DevOps processes.

The Human Factor: Examines the role of people in cybersecurity, focusing on human behavior, social engineering, insider threats, and security awareness. It highlights strategies for fostering a culture of security, training users, and addressing the psychological aspects that attackers exploit.
